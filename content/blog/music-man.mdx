This is a little restoration project I’ve been chipping away at — a 1978 Music Man 210HD 130.

When I picked it up a few months back, it had definitely seen better days. One speaker was seized, the reverb tank was missing in action, and the back panel was gone. The all-original tubes were working… temporarily. One eventually died after a few weeks.

![Sild City Vinylklubb](/images-blog/sild-city.webp)

First order of business was swapping in a fresh matched set of tubes rated for the high voltage this amp runs — E34L-JJs. The speakers (which weren’t even stock, having been replaced around 1980) got swapped out for a pair of Warehouse G10C/S, each rated at 75W to handle the 130W this beast puts out.



I also fitted a new reverb tank and added a set of casters — my back thanks me. To finish it off, I replaced the grillcloth with a Black and Tan from Mojotone. Really happy with how it turned out.

Next up: replacing all the ancient capacitors. ⚡
