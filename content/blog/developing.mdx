*About 6 weeks ago, I parted ways with Doist. And, honestly, it took all of two days before I got antsy and started a new project (😅). There are things to _make_, people! Anyway. Here’s an introduction to [DeepCut](getdeepcut.app). More to come! If you want to be a part of early testing, sign up here.*

Back in December I jotted down a rough concept, a “Goodreads for vinyl”. The working title? Crate Digger. 🪏 It was quickly relegated it to a “someday maybe" corner of my notes while I put my efforts into my day job. 

The idea gathered dust while my attention plowed into an ambitious campaign for Todoist’s voice-to-tasks feature, Ramble (which you should totally [check out](https://www.todoist.com/ramble), btw). 

Fast forward to the end of January. As I made my *exit* from at Doist, I had an itch. I wanted to explore more ambitious LLM workflows. Get beyond automating repetitive work. Stretch outside my comfort zone and see what's possible. Figure out how to **build**.

So, for the past month or so I've been diving deep into the world of "AI-native" software development workflows, and bringing that dusty idea to life under a new name: DeepCut.

## Sild City Vinylklubb
 
 To see where I’m going with DeepCut...
 
 For about two years, I've been meeting up with a group of friends for a monthly vinyl club night here in Haugesund. The four of us each bring a record we're excited about, along with a beer that pairs with the record (of course). We take it in turns to present our pick, giving some background and sharing stories about what makes that particular record so special. 
 
 Then, we listen.
 
 [ Vinyl night pic ]
 
 We don't adhere to any rules. All genres are on the table. The picks that come each month are _wildly_ varied. But the jumping-off point is always the same: we’re there to appreciate the records and the stories behind them (and some tasty beers at the same time 🍻 ).
 
 What started out as a casual hangout has turned into a ritual all four of us look forward to each month. We even gave it a name: _Sild City Vinylklubb_
 
 [ Sild City hat pic ]
 
 So it got me thinking: what would it look like to bring this experience to more people? A way to discover records you'd otherwise never hear about, with rich stories or new-to-you connections to music you already love. And, most importantly, what would it look like to _share_ these musical discoveries with friends in a way that goes deeper than playlists and 30s social media clips. 
 
 Now, if you've ever dipped even semi-seriously into the world of vinyl, you'll probably have heard of [Discogs](https://www.discogs.com). It's _the_ place to manage your collection and buy hard-to-find records. But as great as Discogs is for cataloging and buying records, there’s a gap.
 
 What makes our monthly record club so special is that you get to hear from your friends firsthand what records they're excited about. Not what’s topping the sales charts or dominating Spotify Wrapped roundups. These are albums that connect or tell an interesting story that we can’t help but talk about.
