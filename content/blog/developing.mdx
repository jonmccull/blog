About 6 weeks ago, I parted ways with Doist. And, honestly, it took all of two days before I got antsy (😅). and started work on a new project: [DeepCut](getdeepcut.app).

DeepCut is a passion project that's been rattling around in my head for a few months now. Back in December I'd jotted down a rough concept, relegating it to a "maybe someday" corner of my notes.

At the same time, I've been itching to explore more ambitious LLM workflows. Really stretching outside my area of expertise and seeing what's possible with a little help from Claude *et al*.

So, for the past month or so I've been diving deep into the world of building with AI, and bringing a passion project to life. 

## What is DeepCut 

 First off, I should probably explain _what_ exactly I am setting out to build to give you a sense of the goal here. For about two years, I've been meeting up with a group of friends for a monthly vinyl club night. Each of us brings a record we're excited about, along with a beer that pairs with the record. We take it in turns to present our pick, giving some context and sharing stories about what makes that particular record so special. Then we listen.
 
 We don't adhere to any rules. All genres are on the table. The picks that come each month are wildly varied. But the appreciation for the records (and the stories behind them) are the jumping-off point. No judgement, no preconceptions.
 
 This monthly meetup is easily one of my favorite nights of the month. So it got me thinking: what would it look like to bring this experience to more people? A way to discover records you'd otherwise never have heard of, with rich stories and new-to-you connections to music you already cherish. And, most importantly, what would it look like to _share_ your latest musical discoveries with friends in a way that goes deeper than playlists and 30s social media clips. TL;DR: what would the "Goodreads for vinyl" look like?
 
 Now, if you've ever dipped even semi-seriously into the world of records, you've probably heard of [Discogs](https://www.discogs.com). It's _the_ place to manage your collection and buy hard-to-find records. But as great as Discogs is for cataloging and buying records, there a gap. 
 
 What makes our monthly record club so special is that you get to hear from your friends firsthand what records they're excited about. We
 traverse artists, genres, and eras to discover new music together.

And that's the vision of DeepCut. A way to bring this experience to everyone.
 
 ## Where to start
 To keep the scope of this relatively tight, I decided to push ahead with an iOS app as the first stop on bringing this platform to life. The vinyl-specific app of choice for most collectors, Discogs, has a notoriously patchy experience on mobile. The target audience I'm shooting for is folks who may be new to the world of collecting. Mobile-first seems like the obvious call here. 

## AI-native development 
Like most knowledge workers, I’ve spent plenty the past two years figuring out where AI fits into my work. Tinkering with automations, leaning on LLMs and for big pieces of data analysis, and sitting with my Claude co-pilot throughout the day. But until now my experience with AI has been trying to answer “where it fits with what I *already* do”. How to accelerate and deliver better work, faster.

But from the outset the DeepCut project has been different. It’s the first time I’ve taken on software development in earnest, going (_way_) beyond my day job as a marketer and building a standalone product. I've spent years working alongside teams that can seemingly wizard a vision into the world through their sheer technical brilliance. I understand the workflow of how this happens, but I don't have the technical know-how to actually _build_ the thing myself. And, of course, that's where AI comes in.
